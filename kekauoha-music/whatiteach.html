What I Teach

Conor has been playing and involved with music since he was 7-year-old and has loved most every moment of it! He plays a wide range of styles, ranging from: Classical and Ragtime pieces to Pop and Original Soundtrack music. He has been teaching for 10+ years and has taught a wide variety of students ranging from ages 5 to older than 40! He has the patience and kindness needed to guide young children with their musical journey. He also has the depth of knowledge and experience to bring those who feel stuck to the next level in their playing ability.





Piano

If you are a beginner or intermediate piano player Conor can help take your playing to the next level. For beginner students (mainly young children), he uses a unique teaching style that focuses on developing inner ear balance along with creating a firm foundation for reading musical notation and establish proper technique. For intermediate players, he assesses where you are and what your playing goals are and helps you achieve them!

Violin

Conor can help beginner violin players focus on the fundamentals of violin playing. He uses the Violin Suzuki Method with a mix of pieces from current or modern music to keep playing fun and interesting for each student. While keeping lessons fun, he also focuses on developing proper and lasting technique.





Guitar

If you are a beginner guitar player, Conor is the guy to get you started. He tailors the lessons towards how and what you want to play. While also focusing on the basics of guitar playing, such as: fretboard knowledge, picking/playing styles, and correct form/technique. For those who want to get over that infamous intermediate plateau, Conor can help with that. From learning to comfortably navigate the fretboard, to expanding your music theory knowledge. Both of which are essential to mastering the guitar.

Ukulele

Playing the Ukulele is way easier than it may look! Not only does it have less strings than a Guitar, but it is smaller too! Conor can help you get started by guiding you through learning basic chords, more advance chord shapes, and strumming/picking patterns.
